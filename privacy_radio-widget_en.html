Privacy Policy — Radio Widget Effective 2026-05-26 · Last updated 2026-05-26

This privacy policy describes how the Radio Widget browser extension ("the extension") handles your data. It is published by rheysoft, reachable at rheysoft@gmail.com. The extension is distributed via the Chrome Web Store, Firefox Add-ons (AMO) and the Microsoft Edge Add-ons store.

TL;DR

  • We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal data about you.
  • All your data (favorites, settings, license state) stays inside your browser, in chrome.storage.local. Nothing is sent to a server we operate.
  • The extension talks to three third-party services on your behalf: Radio Browser (community station directory), Gumroad (only for Premium license verification, only when you activate Premium), and individual radio stations (audio stream + station logo).
  • Free version works completely without any account, license key, or third-party API call except Radio Browser.
  • You can delete all locally stored data at any time via Settings → "Clear all local data".

1. What the extension stores locally

The extension uses chrome.storage.local to persist the following inside your browser. Nothing is sent off your device.

You can inspect this data yourself via your browser's DevTools: Application → Storage → chrome.storage.local.

2. What the extension does NOT store or collect

  • No name, no email, no address, no phone number
  • No IP address (we cannot see it; only the third parties you connect to can)
  • No browser fingerprint or device identifier other than the random local machineId (never transmitted)
  • No usage analytics, no telemetry, no crash reports
  • No cookies set by the extension
  • No chrome.storage.sync — nothing syncs across devices

3. Network connections

The extension's Content Security Policy restricts outbound connections to the following destinations:

3.1 Radio Browser (*.radio-browser.info)

A community-operated, open directory of internet radio stations. The extension connects to fetch:

  • Station lists: When you browse or search. Sent: your search query and selected filters (country, genre). Received: list of stations.
  • Country list: When you open the "By country" filter.
  • Play count ping (/json/url/{uuid}): When you start a station. Sent: only the station's UUID. Used by Radio Browser to maintain popularity rankings. No personal data attached.

Radio Browser logs IP addresses and request data on their side, as any web server does. See Radio Browser's website for details.

3.2 Gumroad (api.gumroad.com)

Only contacted when you activate Premium, and every 7 days afterwards to re-verify your license. Sent: your license key and the product ID. Received: a boolean validity flag and purchase metadata.

If Gumroad is unreachable, the extension grants a 14-day offline grace period using its locally cached verification status, then disables Premium until you reconnect.

Gumroad acts as our payment processor and license vendor. See Gumroad's privacy policy for how they handle the data you provide when buying.

3.3 Individual radio stations

Each station you play involves a direct connection between your browser and the station's stream server. The extension does not act as a proxy.

  • Audio stream: The browser makes an HTTP/HTTPS request to the station's stream URL. We set Referrer-Policy: no-referrer, so the extension's identifier is not sent in the Referer header.
  • Station favicon: Same treatment — referrerpolicy="no-referrer" on the <img> element.

Station operators see your IP address (unavoidable for any HTTP connection) and the fact that you connected to their stream. They cannot see that the connection came from this extension.

4. Premium and payment

The Premium upgrade is a one-time purchase processed by Gumroad. The extension never sees your payment details (card number, PayPal account). You receive a license key from Gumroad after purchase; you enter that key in the extension to unlock Premium features.

The same license key unlocks Premium in Chrome, Firefox, and Edge separately — you simply enter it once in each browser.

5. Your rights

You have the right to:

  • Access your data: it's all in chrome.storage.local, viewable via DevTools.
  • Export your favorites: Settings → Favorites → "Export JSON".
  • Erase all your data: Settings → "Clear all local data". This wipes every key the extension uses.
  • Deactivate Premium on a device without losing your license key: Settings → "Deactivate Premium on this device".
  • Object to processing: simply uninstall the extension. We have no data to delete on our side because we don't store any.
  • Lodge a complaint with your data protection authority (in the EU, this would typically be the authority in your country of residence).

To exercise any right that requires action from us, write to rheysoft@gmail.com. Note that because we store no personal data about you, we generally cannot act on access or erasure requests other than confirming this fact.

6. Legal basis (GDPR Art. 6)

Where the extension processes data on your behalf (e.g. fetching station lists from Radio Browser), the legal basis is legitimate interest (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) — namely, providing the functionality you installed the extension for. License verification involves a contractual relationship under Art. 6(1)(b) when you purchase Premium.

We do not rely on consent (Art. 6(1)(a)) because we do not process any data beyond what is strictly necessary to deliver the requested functionality.

7. Children's privacy

The extension is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from children. Since we collect nothing from anyone, this is largely a formality.

8. Data retention

  • Locally stored data: kept until you delete it (or uninstall the extension, which deletes everything).
  • License verification cache: refreshed every 7 days, replaced on each successful verify.
  • Radio Browser logs: governed by their retention policy.
  • Gumroad records: governed by their retention policy.

9. Cross-border transfers

When you connect to Radio Browser or a station's stream server, your browser may exchange data with servers outside your country of residence. The extension itself does not transfer data across borders; the browser does, the same way it would when visiting any website.

Gumroad operates in the United States. By purchasing Premium, you accept that license-verification requests will reach Gumroad's US-based infrastructure.

10. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy when the extension's behavior changes (for example, if new functionality requires a new third-party service). The "Last updated" date at the top reflects the most recent change. We will not retroactively expand data processing for features that previously collected nothing.
